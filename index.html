Why SMP revenue peaked in Jan–Mar 2025 and declined through year-end
The 2025 trajectory — strong H1, declining H2 — reflects SMP's sensitivity to international visitor volume. Santa Monica is a premium destination for European, Canadian, and Asia-Pacific travellers. 09 · Conclusions
What the data says about Namastay at Santa Monica Proper
Finding 01
SMP's CVR decline was an AZDS-era problem that Namastay is beginning to reverse
The conversion deterioration between April 2025 and April 2026 — mobile CVR −65%, desktop CVR −24%, total purchases −29% — happened entirely on AZDS. The cause was audience composition: restaurant, spa, and lifestyle content visitors swelled the session count while booking intent per session fell. Namastay's first 28 days show daily bookings recovering from the April 2026 low, not continuing the decline.
Finding 02
Daily booking rate at 15.0/day — above the April 2026 AZDS baseline
421 confirmed purchases in 28 days (15.0/day) vs 365 in 30 days (12.2/day) on the last AZDS month. On an equivalent monthly basis, Namastay is performing 23% higher than the booking engine it replaced. This is the primary benchmark for assessing early Namastay performance at SMP.
Finding 03
★ Mobile CVR directionally improving — but requires more data to confirm
Mobile CVR moved from 0.19% (April 2026 AZDS) to 0.23% (Namastay). This 21% improvement on the immediate prior period is directionally positive. However, with 28 days of data, no seasonality control, and no server-side export available for SMP, this finding is preliminary. A minimum of 4–6 months of Namastay data compared against the equivalent 2025 period is required before any conclusive statement on mobile conversion trajectory can be made to leadership.
Finding 04
Bot traffic inflated session count and suppressed apparent CVR
6,228 automated sessions from China, Belgium, India, Singapore, and Nigeria in the Namastay window produced zero purchase conversions. These are price-monitoring crawlers and scraper tools — a network-level automated pattern affecting multiple portfolio properties simultaneously. Removing them brings clean booking-intent CVR to approximately 1.78%, substantially closer to the pre-decline AZDS baseline.
Finding 05
Macro headwinds are the dominant market factor — Namastay cannot address them
US inbound international tourism is down 14% YoY. LA County international arrivals are down 30%. SMP's H1 2025 peak under AZDS will not be replicated in the same market environment. The comparison between April 2025 AZDS (when demand was stronger) and May 2026 Namastay (in a materially weaker market) is not a fair test of the booking engine. The correct comparison is Namastay vs the April 2026 AZDS low — which shows a 23% improvement.
10 · Recommendations
Priority actions for Santa Monica Proper
● High Priority
Investigate the root cause of the 2025 AZDS CVR decline
SMP's mobile CVR fell 65% on AZDS between April 2025 and April 2026. Understanding exactly which traffic sources drove the session growth without booking intent — whether paid campaigns, SEO growth on non-booking pages, or social referrals to restaurant content — is essential. Addressing the audience quality issue will compound any Namastay improvements.
● High Priority
Apply bot session filters in GA4
China, Belgium, India, Singapore, and Nigeria produced 6,228 automated sessions in 28 days with near-zero purchases. These suppress CVR metrics and distort the performance baseline. Apply GA4 Admin filters to exclude these traffic clusters. Until removed, all Namastay CVR comparisons for SMP will understate the true booking conversion rate.
● Medium Priority
Establish 90-day Namastay baseline before OKR target-setting
28 days is insufficient for defensible OKR targets. Pull June, July, and August 2026 vs June–August 2025 comparisons when available. This provides seasonality-controlled CVR and revenue benchmarks that can be formally committed to in the OKR framework without risk of revision.
● Medium Priority
Optimise the Namastay mobile experience for the research-to-booking journey
SMP's mobile visitors are largely in a research and inspiration phase — browsing rooms and offers on mobile before converting on desktop. Ensure the Namastay mobile interface makes it easy to save, share, or return to a room selection across devices. Retargeting campaigns that re-engage mobile browsers with a desktop booking prompt can capture cross-device intent that single-session attribution misses.
● Medium Priority
Pull May 2025 AZDS data for true YoY comparison
All current comparisons use April as the AZDS baseline (a different month from Namastay's May window). Pull May 2025 LP purchase data and session data for SMP. This controls for seasonality and provides a like-for-like comparison of Namastay's first month against the equivalent period last year under AZDS.
● Monitor
Track international visitor recovery as a demand tailwind
If US-Canada and US-Europe diplomatic relations stabilise in H2 2026, SMP stands to benefit disproportionately given its destination appeal to international leisure travellers. Monitor Canadian and European booking share monthly. A recovery in this cohort — independent of booking engine — could restore SMP's H1 2025 performance levels.

09 · Conclusions

Finding 01
DTLA delivers the portfolio's clearest early Namastay win
Daily bookings +49%, desktop CVR flat, mobile CVR unchanged, avg booking value +35%, revenue +69% vs last AZDS month. Every measurable indicator is positive. Between April 2025 and April 2026 on AZDS, desktop CVR fell from 1.70% to 1.17% (−31%) and mobile CVR from 0.39% to 0.16% (−59%). Finding 03
Demand environment is DTLA's tailwind — corporate and event-driven demand is growing
DTLA benefits from structural demand drivers that are not present at SMP or ABH: proximity to SoFi Stadium events, a growing downtown corporate corridor, and a guest profile less sensitive to international tourism decline. These demand tailwinds, combined with Namastay's improved booking accessibility, are the two forces driving DTLA's outperformance.
Finding 04
★ 28 days is insufficient for final conclusions — trajectory is strong but needs confirmation
The early DTLA signals are the most positive in the portfolio. However, the same data maturity caveat applies: 28 days through one calendar month, without seasonality control, is not enough to formalise OKR targets or publish definitive performance statements to leadership. Finding 05
DTLA hotel_name inconsistency needs resolution — not a data loss, but a reporting risk
The two hotel_name values for DTLA represent the same property. No bookings are being lost — all 239 are captured when both values are combined. The risk is in automated reporting: any dashboard or report pulling by a single hotel_name value will show incomplete data for DTLA. Namastay should be asked to standardise to one consistent property name.
